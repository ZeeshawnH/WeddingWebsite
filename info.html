  • Hadith-e-Kisa

    The Tradition of the Cloak

    Recited by Mirza Mohammed Kamran

    Hadith-e-Kisa (linked here), also known as the Tradition of the Cloak, is a famous narration in Islamic tradition that recounts a significant event involving the Prophet Muhammad (SAWS), his daughter Fatima (SA), his cousin and son-in-law Ali (AS), and his grandsons Hasan (AS) and Hussain (AS).


    The narration describes an incident where the Prophet Muhammad (SAWS) gathered his family members under a cloak (kisa). As they gathered beneath it, the Prophet Muhammad supplicated to Allah (SWT), invoking blessings and seeking the purification and protection of his family members.


    The narration highlights the spiritual significance and purity of the Prophet Muhammad's (SAWS) family, emphasizing their status as the Ahl al-Bayt (People of the House) who hold a special place in Islam. It serves as a reminder of their sanctity and the importance of following their teachings and example.

  • Manqabat

    Ali ke Chahne Wale

    Those Who Love Ali

    Recited by Mirza Mohammed Salman


    Ali Ke Chahne Wale Khushi Aise Manate Hain

    Lagakar Hyderi naare, hai chehre muskuraatey hai


    Those who love Ali celebrate their happiness in such a way,

    By chanting the slogans of Hyder (referring to Imam Ali), their faces light up with smiles

    Humari Bandagi Maula Hamari Zindagi Maula,

    Nabi Ne Keh Diya Maula Jise Bas Hai Wahi Maula,

    Sikhaya Ma Ne Apni Loriyon Meinya Ali Maula,

    Ghadeer-e-qum Ka Qissa,

    Apne Bachon Ko Sunate Hain,


    Our adoration is for Maula (referring to Imam Ali), our life is for Maula,

    The Prophet declared, "The one who is the foundation is the true Maula,"

    The story of Ghadeer-e-Qum, We narrate it to our children. We narrate to our children the story of Ghadeer-e-Qum


    Khula Karta Hai Hajj Ke Darmiyan Khushiyon Ka Darvaza

    Tawaf Aisa Hai Hota Jhoomta Hai Qana-e-kaba,

    Dilon Meinazmat-e-tauheed Lab Par Ya Ali Maula,

    Khuda Ke Ghar Ali Paida, Khuda Ke Ghar Ali Paida,

    Huwe Sab Ko Batate Hai, Hyder! Hyder! Hyder!


    It opens the door of happiness during the journey of Hajj,

    The circumambulation (Tawaf) of the Kaaba is such that it dances,

    The devotion of monotheism in hearts, the chant of "Ya Ali Maula" on lips,

    Ali is born in the house of God,

    hey proclaim it to everyone, Hyder! Hyder! Hyder!


    Jo Kehlata Hai Momin Karbala Ka Hai Woh Bashinda,

    Mazalim Ke Muqabil Khoon Se Rehta Hai Payinda,

    Ali Par Jan Dekar Ye Hamesha Hogaye Zinda.

    Shaheedon Ke Toh Chehre Bhi, Shaheedon Ke Toh Chehre Bhi

    Kafan Meinmuskurate Hai. Hyder! Hyder! Hyder!


    The one who is called a believer is the inhabitant of Karbala,

    In the face of injustices, they remain alive through their blood,

    By sacrificing their lives for Ali, they have become eternal.

    Even the faces of the martyrs, smile beneath their shrouds.


    Koyi Boodha Bhi Ajaye To Wo Hokar Jawan Uthe,

    Dilon Se Nara-e-hyder Zameen Ta Asman Uthe,

    Mere Har Sher Par Dil Se Munafiq Ke Dhoan Uthe,

    Jo Munkir Hai Ali Ke, Unko Lafzo Se Jalate Ha


    Even if an old person arrives, they rise up rejuvenated,

    From the hearts, the cry of Hyder (referring to Imam Ali) reaches the heavens,

    On every verse of mine, may the smoke of hypocrites dissipate,

    Those who deny Ali, we burn them with our words.


    Musalsal Koshisho Ke Bad Bhi Nakam Tha Lashkar,

    Alam Jisko Payambar Se Mila Woh Mard Hai Hyder

    Mawali Sab Ikathe Hogaye Hai Sajgaya Khaibar,

    Jo Sachi Bat Hoti Hai Wahi Sab Ko Batate Hai.


    Even after continuous efforts, the army was unsuccessful,

    The true lion, Hyder, received the flag from the Prophet.

    All the loyal ones have gathered, Khaibar has been adorned,

    They proclaim only the truth to everyone.
